The Approach

Why psychology changes
your conversion rate

Most marketers optimise for the algorithm. I optimise for the mind reading the ad. My background in psychology (a BA from UCI and an MSc from the University of Edinburgh) isn't a nice credential. It's the lens I use to write every subject line, structure every ad set, and design every experiment.

When I research an audience, I'm not just looking at demographics. I'm mapping their mental models: what they're afraid of, what they tell themselves, what kind of person they want to be. That's where conversion copy actually lives.

  • 01
    Behavioural Segmentation Audiences aren't demographics. They're clusters of beliefs, habits, and triggers. I segment accordingly.
  • 02
    Cognitive Bias Mapping Loss aversion, scarcity, social proof, reciprocity: each campaign deploys the right lever for the right audience moment.
  • 03
    Hypothesis-Driven Testing Every A/B test starts with a behavioural theory, not a gut feeling. That's how results replicate.

About

The marketer who studied
why people change their minds

I started in psychology because I wanted to understand people. I ended up in marketing because it turns out that's where that knowledge does its most interesting work.

After graduating from UCI and completing an MSc in Counselling Studies at Edinburgh, I spent time tutoring, which sharpened my ability to communicate complex ideas simply, handle objections in real time, and read what someone actually needs versus what they say they need. Those are the same skills that make a great CRM strategist or ad copywriter.

I've since worked across B2B and B2C, from London's MVF Global to Bengaluru-based Appu Series, running paid acquisition, outbound email, and content experiments that connect behavioural insight to real revenue. I'm based in Bengaluru, open to remote-first freelance projects globally.
